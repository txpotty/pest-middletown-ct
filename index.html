Ant Infestations

Ants are a ubiquitous problem in Middletown, especially during the warmer months. These persistent insects can invade homes and businesses in search of food and water, quickly establishing large colonies. While most ants are a nuisance, some species, like carpenter ants, can cause structural damage by tunneling through wood. Identifying the type of ant is crucial for selecting the most effective treatment method. We address ant infestations by targeting the colony itself, not just the visible ants, ensuring complete eradication and preventing their return. This often involves baiting or applying treatments to entry points and nesting areas. Understanding their foraging trails and nesting habits helps us pinpoint the source of the infestation and implement the most effective control measures. Ant control requires a thorough approach to prevent them from simply relocating and starting a new colony elsewhere. Our technicians are skilled at tracking ant trails and implementing targeted treatments that eliminate the colony and prevent future issues effectively. The presence of even a few ants can be an indicator of a much larger problem lurking out of sight.

Termite Activity

Termites are a significant threat to properties in Connecticut, causing extensive and costly structural damage. These wood-destroying insects can silently feast on the wooden components of your home or business for years before their activity becomes noticeable. Early detection and treatment are essential to minimize damage. We offer comprehensive termite inspections and effective termite treatment solutions, including baiting systems and liquid barrier treatments, to protect your investment. Our inspections involve looking for signs like mud tubes, damaged wood, and discarded wings. Termite damage can compromise the structural integrity of a building, making prompt action vital. Preventative measures are also key in avoiding future termite infestations. We can advise you on ways to reduce attractants like moisture and wood-to-soil contact. Investing in professional termite control is investing in the long-term health and stability of your property. Don’t wait until you see visible signs; proactive inspections are the best defense against these destructive pests. Termites are a serious concern that requires the expertise of trained professionals.

Flea Problems
